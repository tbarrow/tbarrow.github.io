Supervised Learning Capstone

The intent of this project was to explore the usability and versatility of unsupervised learning for a dataset of my choice. I decided to use a dataset made up of 20,000 pieces of data corresponding to various patterns taken from 800 images of the Avila Bible. The dataset contained a column with twelve values, each pertaining to one of twelve assumed-to-be-present copyists within the bible, labeling which worked on which pattern. I removed this column from my dataset for the sake of unsupervised learning, yet used it to determine the accuracy of my KMeans algorithm for n_cluster values ranging from 8 - 15 through the use of ARI and Silhouette Scores, and additionally for cluster analysis at n_clusters = 12 via a contingency table. I additionally used multiple dimensionality reduction methods to explore just how many clusters we could find visually within our data, fine-tuning the parameters of each for the best results. My overarching research question for this project was, "Just how much will the presence of many outliers in all of our features influence how comparable our clusters are to the actual classes of our data?" As such, the second half of this project required me to individually winsorize each column in our dataset, and then reapply each technique to see what had changed from the original dataset.

About

My name is Trevor, and I am a 2019 graduate from UIUC where I obtained my Bachelor's in Applied Mathematics. I've decided to pursue further training in Data Science post-graduation via Thinkful's Data Science bootcamp such that I will hold more of an edge both skill and experience-wise once I decide to enter the job market. I'm a diligent worker and a quick learner with a passion for mathematics, photography, writing, and problem-solving. I've always had an innate love for puzzles, and see both math and coding as puzzles in their own right, leading me to find great enjoyment in pursuing them. Whether it be manipulating matrices by hand for optimization, experimenting with code to solve a number theory problem, or evaluating an area to determine where I could take the best shots from, I always manage to find great enjoyment in whatever it may be that I pursue. In having a lifelong love for the arts (a love that I nearly chose to pursue at a young age), I believe that my imagination and level of creativity allow me to view technical problems from a unique perspective that many of my peers lack. As such, this allows me to create innovative solutions for issues that may not have been previously considered or explored. When these two aspects are brought together, I've been able to provide to my team a friendly and stress-free, yet productive, mind who is always willing to help out in any way I can. My goal for my career is to eventually work alongside environmentalists and conservationists such that I will be able to aid in the research and analysis of how unique ecosystems are being damaged by both pollution and climate change.
